Research projects

Vision-Based Guidance for Aerial and Ground Robots in Indoor Environment
March 2023 – July 2023
Master's semester project at the Avionics Systems Lab, Anna University - MIT Campus
Supervisors: Dr.A.Saravanakumar

The objective of this project was to plan an autonomous vision-based guidance and landing algorithm for navigation of aerial cum ground robots in an GPS-denied environment. This was achieved through a novel algorithm that accurately uses OpenCV, ArUco markers as a reference for landing and guidance using camera module. The applicability of developed markers was validated using aerial robot landing experiments in a virtual environment (Gazebo Simulator) and ROS (Robot Operating System) platform.

Drive by Wire Control of a Monocopter
July 2021 – March 2022
Bachelor's thesis at the UAV Lab, Periyar Maniammai Institute of Technology
Supervisors: Mr.R.K.Muthukumar

The asymmetric and all-rotating platform requires the development of a novel sensing and control framework. The general rigid body dynamics are separated into rotor dynamics and particle navigation, which are derived for a coordinated helical turn flight path. The equations of motion will be used to calculate the forces necessary for flight along a trajectory recorded with a visual motion capture system. To further realize the monocopter’s potential, a method for controlling its flight must be developed and demonstrated.

Autonomous AI Drone with Real-Time Object Detection
August 2020 – November 2020
Bachelor's semester project at Periyar Maniammai Institute of Technology

An object tracking method is proposed that fuses inertial measurement unit data, global positioning system data, and so the moving object detection results to calculate the relative position between coordinate systems of the thing and therefore the drone. This project presents a replacement lightweight real-time onboard object tracking approach with multi-inertial sensing data, wherein a highly energy-efficient drone is formed supported the flight board. The flight board uses a digital signal processor to grasp PX4 autopilot, an open-source autopilot system oriented toward inexpensive autonomous aircraft. It also uses an ARM core to grasp Linux, robot operating systems, open-source computer vision library, and related algorithms.
